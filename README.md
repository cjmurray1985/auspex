# Preordain — MTG Arena-style Draft Simulator

*Make the pick you were meant to make.*

Practice drafting like it's MTG Arena, then get graded like a pro. Draft the
current Arena limited format against seven bots, build your 40-card deck, and
receive a data-driven grade designed to level up your limited play.

## Run it

```bash
npm install
npm run dev
```

Open http://localhost:5173. The first load fetches card data from Scryfall and
win-rate data from 17lands, then caches both locally.

## How it works

### Data sources

- **[Scryfall](https://scryfall.com/docs/api)** — card data and full card art
  for the current draft set (Marvel Super Heroes / `MSH`).
- **[17lands](https://www.17lands.com/)** — the community-standard limited data:
  - **GIH WR** (games-in-hand win rate) per card, the single best card-quality
    metric, used for cards with ≥500 samples
  - **ALSA** (average last seen at) for cards under the sample floor, mapped
    through a regression fitted on cards that have both signals
  - **Color-pair win rates** for archetype strength
- A rarity/type/text heuristic covers anything 17lands hasn't seen (and makes
  the app work fully offline after the first load).

Card letter grades use 17lands' own methodology: a normal distribution centered
at C, one letter step per 0.33 standard deviations of GIH WR.

### The coach

The goal is **not** to judge whether your final deck is good — it's to judge
whether you made the highest-quality *decision* at each pick given the
information you had. A correct pick that variance turns into a mediocre deck
still scores well; a lucky bomb after poor strategy does not. Everything lives
in `src/coach/` as separable engines (see [Architecture](#architecture)).

Your draft is scored across **seven weighted dimensions of decision quality**,
each computed deterministically and reported with a confidence:

| Category | Weight | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| Card Evaluation | 22% | Did you spot the strongest cards available? |
| Signal Reading | 15% | Did you recognize (and move into) open colors, without over-reacting to noise? |
| Archetype Commitment | 15% | Did you commit at the right time — not too early, not too late, without abandoning a live lane? |
| Deck Cohesion | 15% | Do the picks fit together (creatures, removal, curve, colors)? |
| Staying Open | 13% | Did you keep lanes live through the early picks? |
| Pick Efficiency | 12% | Distribution of best / strong / acceptable / weak / misplay picks |
| Opportunity Cost | 8% | How often did you close doors on more powerful or more flexible options? |

Every pick is re-evaluated **in the context that existed at the time** — the
pool you'd drafted, your color commitment, and the open-color signals read from
the packs you'd seen. Card power itself comes from an **evaluation engine** that
combines multiple data sources (17lands win rate, community pick order, a
card-text heuristic) into a consensus with a *confidence* and a *disagreement*
measure, rather than one opaque number. When strong sources disagree, or when
the top cards are within noise, the pick is marked **contested** and not
penalized — reasonable drafters differ.

### The review

The grade screen is a progressive-disclosure analysis, in the spirit of a
chess.com game review:

- **Overview** — overall grade, a one-line diagnosis, your three biggest
  coaching moments, a category snapshot, and the equity timeline.
- **Categories** — all seven dimensions with confidence and supporting facts.
- **Replay** — every pick, expandable to show the ranked alternatives you
  passed (with power, in-context value, and the reasons each was up or down),
  the signals live at that moment, the opportunity cost, and a coaching note.
- **Timeline** — a **draft-equity** chart (your projected deck strength after
  each pick vs. the best-case line; misplays show as the lines separating) and a
  **commitment meter** (how your colors concentrated, and when that commitment
  became justified).
- **Branches** — the genuine forks where a real alternative pointed at a
  different archetype, each line projected against the pool you ultimately saw.
- **Deck** — the final deck evaluated separately (power, curve, interaction,
  threats, consistency) with plausible alternative decks from the same seat.

All coaching prose is generated by a swappable **explanation layer**: the
engines produce structured *facts*; the narrator only phrases them. The default
narrator is deterministic and offline. An LLM may be dropped in to narrate — but
never to decide the optimal pick.

### Deck builder

An Arena-style two-pane builder — the app never auto-picks your deck, you build
it from your drafted pool:

- **Top pane:** your full pool as a filterable grid (color pips, rarity pips,
  card-type dropdown, name search, sort by mana value / color / rarity). Cards
  already in the deck are dimmed with a check. Unused pool cards are your
  **sideboard**, just like Arena limited.
- **Bottom pane:** the selected deck as a stacked mana curve (columns 0–6+),
  cards overlapping Arena-tight. A lands panel holds any nonbasic lands plus
  basic-land steppers with real art, and an "Auto lands" button fills basics
  to 40 proportional to your spells' pips.
- **Drag & drop** between panes (with drop-glow affordances) or just click.
- Hovering any card shows an enlarged preview plus **keyword reminder boxes**
  (Flying, Power-up, Teamwork, Connive, …) in the spirit of Arena's rules text.
- The grade is computed only after you hit **Done** at 40+ cards.

### Draft simulation

- 8-seat pod, 3 packs × 14 picks, alternating pass direction
- Arena-style play boosters: 1 rare/mythic (1:7 mythic), 3 uncommons,
  9 commons, 1 weighted wildcard slot
- 50-second pick timer that auto-picks (like Arena) if it expires

### Bot personas

Each of the 7 opponents is a randomized **rank × playstyle** persona, so every
pod is a heterogeneous table like real Arena. The strategies are synthesized
from three open-source drafters:

- **[dr4ft](https://github.com/dr4fters/dr4ft)** — its bot picks purely at
  random. That's our low-rank floor: high evaluation noise plus a "blunder"
  chance to grab a shiny/random card.
- **[RyanSaxe/mtg](https://github.com/RyanSaxe/mtg)** — a 17lands-trained net
  that reached Mythic #27. Contributes the "stay open early, commit later"
  ramp and archetype/synergy bias.
- **[nemedraft-client](https://github.com/negaga53/nemedraft-client)** — trained
  on 17lands trophy (7-win) decks. Contributes explicit **signal reading**
  (detecting which colors are open from what's flowing).

**Rank** (Bronze → Mythic) scales evaluation accuracy, signal-reading, color
discipline, and blunder rate. In simulation, early-pick accuracy runs ~87%
(Bronze) → ~99% (Mythic), versus ~68% for pure-random. **Playstyle** (Power,
Color Forcer, Synergy, Signal Reader, Rare Drafter, Aggro) shapes *how* they
draft — e.g. rare-drafters hoard bombs, forcers lock two colors immediately,
signal-readers move into open colors. Your table is shown on the grade screen.

### Atmosphere

A hi-res art backdrop plus a light particle layer sit behind the UI:

- **Background** — full-bleed illustrations sourced from
  [mtgpics.com](https://www.mtgpics.com/art?set=493) at ~1500–3800px (far
  sharper than Scryfall's ~626px art crops), cross-fading between the set's
  mythics and rares with a slow Ken Burns drift. mtgpics uses the same
  collector numbers as Scryfall, so the URL is derived directly from each card.
  Missing art (404) is skipped automatically.
- **Dark, color-tinted scrim** (deep navy/indigo with a warm floor — not grey)
  keeps foreground UI legible; it dims hard during drafting/building so cards
  pop, and opens up on the menu and grade screens so the art breathes.
- **Rising embers** on a lightweight 2D canvas, with per-phase moods (warm gold
  on the menu, sparse cool motes while drafting, a dense swirl while grading)
  plus delight bursts: a flare on each new pack, a gold burst on mythic picks,
  a spark fountain synced to the grade letter, and an encore on a personal best.

The whole atmosphere adds ~0 KB of dependencies (no 3D engine) and the app
shell is ~117 KB gzipped.

## Architecture

The coaching system (`src/coach/`) is deliberately modular so new data sources
and even a different explanation model can be plugged in without rewriting
anything else. Data flows one direction:

```
providers ─▶ evaluation ─┐
                          ├─▶ decision ─▶ categories / moments / equity /
picks ─────▶ context ─────┘              commitment / branches / deck
                                                     │
facts ───────────────────────────────────▶ narrate (LLM-swappable) ─▶ UI
```

| Module | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| `providers/` | Independent data sources (`WinRateProvider`, `PickOrderProvider`, `HeuristicProvider`), each returning a power estimate + confidence. Add a source by implementing `EvalProvider`. |
| `evaluation.ts` | Combines providers into a consensus power, a confidence band, and a disagreement measure. Source-agnostic. |
| `roles.ts` | Deterministic functional classification (removal, evasion, card advantage, …). |
| `context.ts` | Reconstructs the draft state at every pick from the recorded log (pool, color commitment, open-color signals, wheel). |
| `decision.ts` | Scores each pick *in context* against its alternatives — tier, value gap, confidence, opportunity cost. |
| `categories.ts` | The seven weighted decision-quality categories → overall grade. |
| `moments.ts` | Ranks the 3–5 highest-impact decisions as coaching lessons. |
| `equity.ts` | Draft-equity timeline and commitment meter. |
| `branch.ts` | Surfaces the genuine archetype forks (not a brute-force tree). |
| `deck.ts` | Final-deck analysis + plausible alternative decks from the same seat. |
| `narrate.ts` | The **only** place natural language is produced. Ships a deterministic `TemplateExplainer`; `setExplainer()` swaps in an LLM adapter. The engines supply facts; the narrator never decides anything. |
| `review.ts` | Orchestrator — runs every engine and assembles one `DraftReview`. |
| `persistence.ts` | Persistence layer. Stores a compact decision-quality `DraftRecord` per draft (no card blobs), migrates the old history, and never computes coaching — it only remembers. |
| `profile.ts` | Progression engine. Turns the record history into a persistent Draft Rating, per-dimension trends, recurring habits, color-pair mastery, streaks, achievements and weekly goals — the long-term personal coach. |

### Long-term coaching

The review's **Progress** tab (and the menu's *Your Coach* view) are driven by
`profile.ts`. A **Draft Rating** (a recency-weighted function of decision
quality, mapped to ranks Novice → Master Drafter) rewards *improvement*, not
play frequency. The engine detects **recurring habits** across your recent
drafts ("you commit too early", "you undervalue interaction") and turns each
into a specific weekly goal; tracks **color-pair mastery**; and unlocks
**achievements**. Because grading is decision-quality based, the rating rises as
you make better choices even when a given deck runs hot or cold.

The draft simulation (`src/engine/`), data fetching (`src/data/`), and the
review UI (`src/components/review/`) sit around this core.

## Stack

React 19 + TypeScript + Vite, zustand for state, framer-motion for UI
animations, Babylon.js for the atmosphere layer. No backend — 17lands calls go
through the Vite dev-server proxy because the `color_ratings` endpoint doesn't
send CORS headers.
